# Appendix E: Glossary of Scientific Terms

## Basic Terms (Ages 3-6)

| Term | Definition | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Observe** | To look carefully and notice things | "I observe that the leaf is green" |
| **Predict** | To guess what will happen | "I predict the ball will roll down" |
| **Compare** | To see how things are the same or different | "This stone is bigger than that one" |
| **Sort** | To put things in groups | "I sorted the seeds by color" |
| **Measure** | To find out how big or heavy something is | "The stick is 10 cm long" |
| **Record** | To write or draw what you found | "I drew the plant in my notebook" |
| **Experiment** | To test an idea | "Let's do an experiment to find out" |

## Intermediate Terms (Ages 7-8)

| Term | Definition | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Hypothesis** | An educated guess that can be tested | "If I add more water, then the plant will grow faster" |
| **Variable** | Something that can change in an experiment | "Temperature is the variable I'm changing" |
| **Control** | Keeping something the same to make a fair test | "I used the same amount of soil in each pot" |
| **Data** | Information collected during an experiment | "My data shows the plant grew 2 cm" |
| **Evidence** | Facts that support or disprove an idea | "The evidence shows my hypothesis was wrong" |
| **Conclusion** | What you learned from an experiment | "I conclude that plants need light to grow" |
| **Trial** | One test in an experiment | "I did three trials and averaged the results" |

## Advanced Terms (Ages 9-10)

| Term | Definition | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Independent variable** | The variable you change on purpose | "I changed the amount of fertilizer" |
| **Dependent variable** | The variable you measure | "I measured how tall the plant grew" |
| **Controlled variable** | Variables you keep the same | "I kept water amount, light, and soil the same" |
| **Mean (average)** | Sum of values divided by number of values | "The mean height was 15 cm" |
| **Range** | Difference between highest and lowest values | "The range was 5 cm" |
| **Reliability** | How consistent and trustworthy results are | "I did 5 trials to improve reliability" |
| **Peer review** | Having others check your work | "My classmates reviewed my experiment" |
| **Replication** | Repeating an experiment to verify results | "Another group replicated my experiment" |

## Meghalaya-Specific Safety

### Monsoon Safety

| Hazard | Prevention | Response |
|--------|------------|----------|
| Flash floods | Monitor weather; avoid streams during heavy rain | Move to high ground immediately |
| Slippery surfaces | Non-slip footwear; careful movement | First aid for falls |
| Lightning | Shelter during storms; avoid open areas | If caught outside, crouch low |
| Leeches | Leech socks; regular checks; salt available | Remove with salt; clean wound |

### Wildlife Safety

| Hazard | Prevention | Response |
|--------|------------|----------|
| Snakes | Watch where stepping; stick to cleared paths | Don't approach; identify if possible; seek medical help if bitten |
| Stinging insects | Avoid disturbing nests; check area before sitting | Remove stinger; monitor for allergic reaction |
| Unknown plants | No tasting; wash hands after handling | If reaction occurs, note plant appearance; seek medical help |

### Cave Safety

| Rule | Reason |
|------|--------|
| Always with adults | Navigation, emergency response |
| Stay on marked paths | Avoid falls, getting lost |
| Carry lights | Caves are completely dark |
| Tell someone your plan | Rescue can find you if needed |
| Don't touch formations | Preservation and safety |

# Appendix H: Troubleshooting Guide

## Common Teaching Challenges

### Challenge: Students Won't Ask Questions

| Possible Cause | Solution |
|----------------|----------|
| Fear of being wrong | Model wondering aloud; celebrate all questions |
| Previous negative experiences | Create safe environment; respond positively to every question |
| Don't know how | Teach question starters; provide question frames |
| Content not interesting | Follow student interests; connect to their lives |

**Quick Fix:** Start every session with teacher modeling: "I wonder why..." and wait for students to add their own.

---

### Challenge: Experiments "Fail"

| Situation | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Didn't get expected result | "What did happen? That's data too!" |
| Procedure went wrong | "What can we learn from this? Let's try again" |
| Students frustrated | Share failure stories; normalize iteration |
| Equipment malfunction | Have backup activities; use failure as learning |

**Key Message:** "In science, we don't really failâ€”we just find out what doesn't work. That's valuable information!"

---

### Challenge: Wide Range of Abilities

| Strategy | How to Implement |
|----------|------------------|
| Tiered activities | Same theme, different complexity levels |
| Peer teaching | Pair advanced with developing students |
| Choice boards | Multiple entry points to same concept |
| Open-ended investigations | Each child works at own level |
| Flexible grouping | Group by interest, not just ability |

---

### Challenge: Limited Materials

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| No budget | See Zero-Cost Materials list (Appendix A) |
| Can't find specific item | Identify substitute or adapt activity |
| Not enough for everyone | Rotate through stations; use group work |
| Items break frequently | Teach proper handling; use more durable alternatives |

---

### Challenge: Time Constraints

| Strategy | Implementation |
|----------|----------------|
| Integrate with other subjects | Math through measurement; language through reports |
| Use transitions | Science questions during lineup, snack |
| Shorten activities | Focus on one skill at a time |
| Extend over days | Multi-day investigations |
| Home connections | Send experiments home |

---

### Challenge: Behavior During Science

| Issue | Response |
|-------|----------|
| Overexcitement | Channel energy; set clear expectations; use calming signals |
| Grabbing materials | Practice turn-taking; establish material procedures |
| Not following safety rules | Review rules; consequences for violations; re-teach |
| Off-task behavior | Increase engagement; check difficulty level; provide choices |

---

### Challenge: Assessment Overwhelm

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Too much documentation | Use Flash Assessment system (Vol 8, Ch 35) |
| Don't know what to look for | Use observation checklists |
| Can't observe everyone | Rotate focus students weekly |
| Results not useful | Focus on actionable observations |

## Common Experiment Problems

### Water Experiments

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Water won't mix with oil | Density difference | This IS the learning! |
| Food coloring spreads unevenly | Temperature variation | Use consistent temperature |
| Plants not growing | Various | Check light, water, drainage |
| Evaporation too slow | Low temperature/humidity | Use warmer location; shallower container |

### Plant Experiments

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Seeds not germinating | Old seeds; wrong conditions | Fresh seeds; check moisture/temperature |
| Mold growing | Too much moisture | Reduce watering; improve drainage |
| Plants dying | Over/under watering; light issues | Adjust conditions systematically |
| Growth too slow to observe | Normal for some plants | Choose fast-growing varieties |

### Physical Science Experiments

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Circuits not working | Connection issues | Check all connections; try fresh batteries |
| Magnets seem weak | Distance; interference | Closer contact; remove metal objects |
| Ramps not consistent | Surface friction | Smooth, consistent surface |
| Measurements varying | Technique issues | Standardize measurement procedure |
